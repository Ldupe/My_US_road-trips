Bonneville Salt Flats
✅
★ ★ ★
Completely surreal, Mars-like landscape, incredible for photos. Easy stop on the way.

🚐 Access: Fully accessible. Paved access road right off I-80, large parking area.

Antelope Island
See Bisons
✅
★ ★ ★
Bison roaming free + Great Salt Lake views, genuinely wild and unique experience.

🚐 Access: Fully accessible. The causeway road to the island is paved, large campgrounds available on the island.
🌀
Spiral Jetty
See / swim Pink lake and salt flats, Train Monument
⚠️
★ ★ ★
Fascinating land art, but requires 2.5h from SLC on rough roads — only if you appreciate art/photography.

🚐 Access: The 16-mile dirt road is extremely bumpy. Smaller RVs can make it in dry conditions but it is slow and rough (~45 min each way), with limited space to turn around. Go only in dry weather — consider a rental car if available.

✅

Beautiful but you'll see more dramatic landscapes elsewhere on this trip.

🚐 Access: Accessible. The scenic canyon road is paved. In winter, chains may be required. RV parking at ski areas can be tight.

Homestead Crater hot spring
✅
★ ★ ★
Completely unique — a geothermal hot spring inside a 55-foot ancient limestone dome you can swim or scuba dive in. Nothing like this exists anywhere else.

🚐 Access: Fully accessible. Located in the town of Midway on paved roads.

★ ★ ★
If visiting in winter, spectacular. Skip in other seasons.

🚐 Access: Fully accessible. Paved parking in Midway.

Fifth Water Hot Springs
⚠️
★ ★ ★
Beautiful colored hot springs in a canyon, worth the hike.

🚐 Access: The trailhead at Diamond Fork Canyon is reachable via a paved but narrow road. Your motorhome can reach the trailhead parking lot. Then it's a 5-mile round trip hike to the hot springs.
🏜️ Moab
🪨
Arches National Park
Delicate Arch, Devils Garden to Landscape Arch, Double Arch, stargazing
✅
★ ★ ★
2,000+ natural arches, utterly unique on earth.

🚐 Access: Fully accessible. Paved scenic drive throughout. The entrance road has a steep grade — go slow but no issues for a 24ft Class C.
🏜️
Canyonlands National Park
Island in the Sky (easy), Needles (remote), Dead Horse Point, Mesa Arch (TROU/VUE INCROYABLE - BEST SUNRISE ! no permit, short hike, 30-45' MIN before sunrise for parking/good spot, in island of the sky), Shafer Trail Road (4WD, no atv), atv rental?
✅
★ ★ ★
Vast and dramatic — Mesa Arch sunrise is one of the most photographed spots in the world.

🚐 Access: Mostly accessible. Island in the Sky and Needles districts are paved and RV friendly. The Maze district is 4WD only — skip it.
🐴
Potash Road Scenic Drive
✅
★ ★ ★
Jaw-dropping Colorado River overlook, easy add-on to Canyonlands.

🚐 Access: Fully accessible. Dead Horse Point State Park is paved. Potash Road (UT-279) runs along the Colorado River — fully paved and scenic.
👒 Mexican Hat
🗿
Valley of the Gods
Similar Monument Valley
⚠️
★ ★ ★
Monument Valley vibes with zero crowds, completely free, spectacular.

🚐 Access: Manageable for Class C motorhomes up to 24ft in dry conditions. Near Castle Rock there are steep tight turns requiring very slow careful maneuvering. Do not attempt after rain — road becomes extremely slick.
🚗
Moki Dugway
Scary steep road ⚠️ no cells ⚠️ check weather/rain- Muley Point overlook, Gooseneck State Park, Valley of the Gods
❌
★ ★ ★
One of the most dramatic switchback roads in the US, incredible views at the top.

🚐 Access: Signs explicitly state 'Not recommended for RVs, buses, and vehicles over 10,000 lbs.' Your motorhome at 12,500 lbs GVWR exceeds the limit. Drive it in a rental car only. Views from the top can also be reached via UT-95 from the north on paved road.
🌉
Natural Bridges National Monument
✅
★ ★ ★
Often skipped, genuinely impressive and far less crowded than Arches.

🚐 Access: Fully accessible. 9-mile paved scenic loop. The park advises caution for vehicles over 26ft — your 24ft is fine.

Scenic Drive, Cassidy Arch, Fruita Orchards
✅
★ ★ ★
The most underrated of Utah's Mighty 5, stunning and uncrowded.

🚐 Access: Fully accessible. Paved scenic drive through the main corridor. Backcountry roads are 4WD only — stick to the main paved areas.

Moonscape Overlook
Hanksville area
⚠️
★ ★ ★
Eerie, remote, spectacular views.

🚐 Access: Dirt road, doable in dry conditions for 2WD driven very slowly. Never attempt after rain. Alternative: a guided tour is available from ~$116/person that eliminates the navigation challenge entirely.
🌈
Bentonite Hills
⚠️
★ ★ ★
Pastel colored hills, very photogenic and unusual.

🚐 Access: Same dirt road conditions as Moonscape Overlook — manageable in perfectly dry conditions only. Never attempt after rain.
🏭
Factory Butte
⚠️
★ ★ ★
Dramatic isolated butte in a moonscape, great for off-road.

🚐 Access: The main road to Factory Butte viewpoint is maintained gravel, passable for 2WD in dry conditions. Conditions change rapidly with weather — never attempt after rain as bentonite clay becomes undrivable even in 4WD.
☀️
Temple of the Sun and Moon, Glass mountain
❌
★ ★ ★
Stunning towers and gypsum crystal made tower (short drive away) in Capitol Reef backcountry

🚐 Access: Deep in Capitol Reef backcountry via Cathedral Valley Road — requires high-clearance 4WD and multiple river crossings. Park your motorhome in Torrey or Hanksville and rent a Jeep, or book a Cathedral Valley guided tour (~$150–200/person).

Consistently ranked one of the most scenic drives in the entire US — you'll drive through it anyway.

AWD ? - Hole-in-the-Rock Road, Peek-a-Boo and spooky gulch slot canyon 3.5mi non technical- depart Dry Fork Trailhead, Lower Calf Creek Falls 6mi 500ft el.g., Devil's Garden, escalamte river trail to escalante natural bridge 4mi river crossing flat Big Horn canyon (anteloppe mais moins bien et long et access bizarre) - Wild Camping

Zion National Park
The Narrows, Angels Landing, Canyon Overlook, Angels Landing, The Subway (permit+maps?navigation system required, check flash flood forecast, "Top-down" 9miles + short rapelling gear needeed, "Bottom-Up" still streneous - permits : 1. apply for dates few months in advance 2.unclaimed permit dec/mar/jun/sept 15 3. Daily lottery 2 days in adavnce - Best season : fall?summer)
⚠️
★ ★ ★
One of the most stunning parks in the US. Non-negotiable.

🚐 Access: Accessible but important caveat: private vehicles over 11ft 4in are prohibited in the Zion-Mt. Carmel tunnel on UT-9. Your motorhome at ~11ft is borderline — measure your exact height. If over the limit, a tunnel escort is available ($15). In season, park at the visitor center and use the free shuttle for Zion Canyon.

Slot canyon into crazy narrow slot canyon
⚠️

🚐 Access: The 8-mile House Rock Valley Road to the trailhead is unpaved. Your motorhome's low clearance makes it risky — consider parking on the US-89 shoulder where some large vehicles stop, or leave the motorhome in Kanab/Page and drive a rental car.

Grand Canyon National Park
+ North Rim, Colorado River canoe
✅
★ ★ ★
Non-negotiable. One of the 7 natural wonders of the world.

🚐 Access: Fully accessible. Both South and North Rim have RV parking and campgrounds. The South Rim shuttle system handles in-park movement — park the motorhome at the visitor center.
💙
Havasupai Falls
$455 permit - eau belle, tunnel hike, waqterfall, creek Havasu, Mooney, Beaver Falls
❌
★ ★ ★
Arguably the most beautiful waterfall in the USA. Life-changing turquoise water. Worth every penny of the $455 permit.

🚐 Access: Not accessible by vehicle — the last 10 miles is hiking trail only. Park at Hualapai Hilltop (paved road, large parking lot) then hike 10 miles in. Alternative: helicopter tours from Hualapai Hilltop (~$500/person).
🔴
Sedona, Coconino National Forest
Devil's Hollows (aka soldier's pass cave, trou/grotte), Bear Mountain, Cathedral Rock (vue pillier rochers, steep hike), Robber's Roost (trou/vue, aka Hideout Cave / Shaman’s Cave), West Fork Trail (hike in water), Birthing Cave (⚠️ weird nav, download map), Little Horse Trail (vue cailloux), Mount Wilson (vue panoramique) Creeks, Devil's bridge from day creek road trailhead (arche). NOTE : trailheads fill out fast, Free shuttle
✅
★ ★ ★
One of the most beautiful landscapes in the USA — vortexes, red rocks, incredible hikes. Non-negotiable.

🚐 Access: Fully accessible. All main roads paved. Uptown Sedona has dedicated RV parking areas.
🔴 Sedona
🌀
Salome Jug
Swimming canyon, slot canyon, toboggan
❌
★ ★ ★
One of the most unique swimming experiences in Arizona — natural waterslides in a pink slot canyon.

🚐 Access: Very remote, requiring a long rough dirt road then a canyon hike involving swimming. Park your motorhome in Globe or Young and rent a high-clearance vehicle, or book a guided tour from the Phoenix/Globe area.
🐚
Fossil Creek
No permits in winter
⚠️
★ ★ ★
One of only two Wild & Scenic rivers in AZ. Crystal clear turquoise water in the desert, very unique.

🚐 Access: Trailhead requires a permit and ~14 miles of rough dirt road. A 24ft motorhome would struggle. Best option: rent a Jeep in Payson or join a shuttle tour. Several outfitters in the Payson area offer Fossil Creek shuttles.
